Thomas Leo Clancy Jr. was an American novelist.
He is best known for his technically detailed espionage and
military-science storylines set during and after the Cold War.
Seventeen of his novels have been bestsellers and more than 100
million copies of his books have been sold.

Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six (often shortened to Rainbow
Six or R6) is a tactical first-person shooter video
game series by Red Storm Entertainment and Ubisoft.
Based on the novel Rainbow Six by American author
Tom Clancy, it revolves around a fictional international
counterterrorist organization called "Rainbow".

Throughout his career, Clancy continued to write bestsellers
featuring Jack Ryan and other recurring characters, including
John Clark, a former Navy SEAL turned CIA operative. His novels
were known for their intricate plots, detailed technical descriptions,
and realistic portrayals of military and intelligence operations.
Clancy's ability to anticipate and accurately depict geopolitical events
earned him a reputation as a "Nostradamus of national security."

Tom Clancy's Jack Ryan (also known simply as Jack Ryan)
is an American political action thriller television
series based on characters from the fictional "Ryanverse"
created by Tom Clancy. Replacing the characters of the novels
with those set in modern time, it depicts CIA analyst Jack
Ryan who is played by John Krasinski. The series was created
by Carlton Cuse and Graham Roland. Cuse and Krasinski serve as
executive producers alongside, Michael Bay, and Mace Neufeld, among others.
